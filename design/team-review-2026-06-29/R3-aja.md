# R3 — Aja Williams, Creative Director (convergence)

## Shared diagnosis

We've actually converged, and not on a fudge. Three things the whole room now holds: **the native voice is a first-class engine, not a crash handler** — Sloan/Han proved it lies in the data (`failed:true` on a perfect Daniel line, no lip-sync envelope), and that gets fixed regardless of any other call. **The signature is the spine** — "Pushed, Sir." is both the brand hero and the activation aha; the story pair and I banked that, and nobody fought it. And **the curtain is already torn** — the exhausted-credit banner, the red "Exhausted" badge, the silent-fail-on-empty-quota already tell the operator Caldwell has a credit meter. That last one is the fact that moves me off my R1 absolutism, and I'm not going to pretend it doesn't.

## My top concession

I conceded my R1 line — *the operator never sees an engine control* — and I'll say plainly what changed my mind and what it costs. The design pair (and Devi, with the same blade) caught me defending an illusion the product had already broken. You cannot protect a curtain that's lying on the floor with an exhausted-credit banner stapled to it. Worse, my "invisible swap" *creates* the failure I most fear: if Daniel isn't installed and the engine flips silently, Caldwell goes mute with no recovery home, and a mute butler is a dead one. So **I concede the control is visible — on one condition that does the real work: it is framed as cost and privacy, never as voice quality.** "Use ElevenLabs — off means free, local, no credits." Marcus's toggle-not-segmented grammar is the UI proof: character is a segmented choice the operator owns; engine is a system switch he glances at. What I give up: the purist's "one identity, zero seams." The cost: a sophisticated user now knows two engines exist, and on a bad day will wonder which mouth is moving. I'll wear that — because the alternative cost (a silent mute with nowhere to go) is worse for the brand than a visible, honestly-labelled fallback. A graceful seam beats a hidden failure.

## My line in the sand

**One name, one face, one canon — the word "Daniel" never reaches the operator, and the engine is never framed as a quality tier.** The toggle may exist; "ElevenLabs / Mac voice" as a labelled *voice-picker* may not. The user toggles *cost and privacy*, never *who Caldwell is*. The instant the surface says "premium voice vs free voice," the portrait above it becomes a lie and the retention story — *he learns your rhythm, he becomes your butler* — dies, because "your Caldwell" cannot cohere if his voice is visibly a dropdown. Same name, same face, same "Sir," whichever engine speaks. That is non-negotiable and survives the concession intact.

## My vote — the 3 principles we ship against

1. **One identity, engine honest-but-not-headlined.** The operator controls cost/privacy, never voice-quality-as-tier. Name and face are constant across both engines. (The concession and the line, fused into a shippable rule.)
2. **Canonise the signature.** "Pushed, Sir." and the seeded canon set promoted from petty-cash fallback to *the named house phrases* — pre-loaded, the download-page demo, the first breath. Brand hero and funnel aha are the same asset; ship it as such.
3. **The bake-off gates the brand claim.** Daniel Enhanced must clear a hard bar — RP register survives, the 95/5 expletive lands crisp, audio-tag intent approximated — *before* we let any caption say "Caldwell sounds like Caldwell either way." If it fails, the toggle stays but the caption tells the truth ("free, different register"), and ElevenLabs stays the one true voice. Honesty is cheaper than a brand lie.

## One open question R3 hasn't resolved

We agreed the engine flips per-line or on a credit crunch — but **nobody has committed that the flip is imperceptible mid-utterance-stream.** If Caldwell speaks three turn-end pings in a session and pings one and three are ElevenLabs while two is Daniel, can the *ear* tell? Sloan/Han can guarantee the portrait moves and the history is honest — they have not guaranteed the *timbre is continuous*. If a listener hears the voice change between two lines a minute apart, the "one identity" principle is cosmetically true and experientially false, and my whole concession was priced on a continuity I haven't seen proven. That's the last thing standing between "shippable" and "shipped."

— Aja
